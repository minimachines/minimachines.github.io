Peter the Great was the 1st Czar of Russia. He was known to be tall, strong, healthy, and have a small moustache. He even visited Holland where he learned to build boats. When he arrived back in Russia, he taught workers. The customs and and building where the boats were made still stand in St.Petersburg Russia.



Customs in St. Petersburg, Russia


Surprisingly, Peter owned 2 houses on each side of the main river, Neva. One bedroom, one kitchen, and the third was his work shop. Now only one wall of his house stands. Of course, he also had the winter and summer palace, peterhof, and many more.



Winter palace


In this image, not only do you see the winter palace but the column with an angel looking over Russia. This column is actually not connected to the ground. It is only held down by its own weight. The winter palace also faces a yellow building where generals and ministers lived. They would run across to the palace to speak with the Czar.



Peter lived a long life from 1672 to 1725.
